Complete list of major preventive screenings (USPSTF/CDC A & B)

General adult population

  • Blood pressure: all adults ≥18 years; check every visit or at least every 3–5 years (annually if risk factors).
  • BMI/obesity: all adults; calculate height, weight and BMI at each visit.
  • Lipid panel: men ≥35 years and women ≥45 years (earlier if risk factors); every 5 years.
  • Diabetes/prediabetes: adults 35–70 years with overweight/obesity; screen every 3 years (fasting glucose or A1c).
  • Colorectal cancer: adults 45–75 years; FIT annually, FIT‑DNA every 3 years, CT colonography every 5 years or colonoscopy every 10 years.
  • Depression: all adults; screen periodically (PHQ‑2/PHQ‑9).
  • Alcohol misuse: all adults; screen periodically (AUDIT‑C, CAGE‑AID).
  • Tobacco use: all adults; ask at each visit and provide counseling/cessation support.
  • Hepatitis C: adults 18–79 years; test once (repeat if ongoing risk).
  • HIV: adults 15–65 years; test once (repeat if risk).
  • Syphilis/STI screening: risk‑based (men who have sex with men, multiple partners, pregnancy); test annually or as indicated.
  • Hepatitis B: adults at risk (born in endemic areas, injection drug use, immunosuppressed); test once.
  • Vaccinations: review immunization status at each visit and update per CDC schedule.

Women’s screening tests

  • Cervical cancer: women 21–29: Pap every 3 years; women 30–65: HPV test every 5 years or Pap every 3 years or co‑test every 5 years.
  • Breast cancer: women 40–74 years: mammography every 2 years.
  • Osteoporosis: women ≥65 or younger women with risk factors: DEXA scan every 1–2 years.
  • Chlamydia & gonorrhea: sexually active women <25 or older at risk: annual NAAT testing.
  • Intimate partner violence: all women of reproductive age: screen periodically.
  • Pregnancy‑related screens: during pregnancy follow schedule for HIV, hepatitis B, syphilis, Rh blood type, group B strep, glucose challenge, etc.

Men’s screening tests

  • Abdominal aortic aneurysm: men 65–75 years who ever smoked: one‑time ultrasound.
  • Prostate cancer (PSA): men 55–69 years: shared decision; interval varies.
  • Testicular cancer: routine screening not recommended.
  • STI screening: risk‑based for HIV, syphilis, gonorrhea and chlamydia.

Smokers or former smokers

  • Lung cancer: adults 50–80 years with ≥20 pack‑year history who smoke or quit <15 years ago: annual low‑dose chest CT.
  • AAA: men 65–75 years who ever smoked: one‑time ultrasound.
  • Cessation counseling: all tobacco users: behavioral or pharmacologic support at every visit.

Older adults (≥65 years)

  • Osteoporosis: women ≥65 and men with risk factors: DEXA every 1–2 years.
  • Cognitive impairment: adults ≥65: screen periodically (MMSE, MoCA).
  • Fall risk: adults ≥65: assess gait and balance at each visit.
  • Vision and hearing: adults ≥65: screen every 1–2 years (Snellen, audiometry).
  • Depression & functional status: adults ≥65: assess mood and activities of daily living at each visit.

Young adults (18–39 years)

  • Cervical cancer: women 21–29: Pap every 3 years.
  • STI screening: sexually active adults: annual chlamydia/gonorrhea/HIV testing based on risk.
  • Blood pressure: adults ≥18: check at each visit.
  • Lipid panel: men ≥20 if high risk: every 5 years.
  • Mental health: depression and anxiety screening periodically.
  • Substance use: screen for alcohol and drug misuse at each visit.
  • Vaccines: ensure up‑to‑date immunizations (HPV, Tdap, influenza, COVID‑19, etc.).

Cancer‑specific summary

  • Colorectal cancer: start at 45 years; continue through 75 years; see intervals above.
  • Breast cancer: women 40–74 years: mammogram every 2 years.
  • Cervical cancer: women 21–65 years: Pap/HPV testing per age group.
  • Lung cancer: age 50–80 with ≥20 pack‑years and smoking history: annual LDCT.
  • Prostate cancer: men 55–69 years: PSA testing based on shared decision making.
  • Skin cancer: no routine screening; visual skin exams for high‑risk patients.

Additional risk‑based or special populations

  • Hepatitis B: screen people born in endemic areas, injection drug users and the immunosuppressed (HBsAg, anti‑HBs, anti‑HBc).
  • Tuberculosis: screen high‑risk individuals (immigrants from endemic regions, exposure or immunocompromise) with IGRA or PPD.
  • Lead/heavy metal: test those with occupational exposure or certain immigrants.
  • Thyroid disorders: no routine screening; test TSH when symptoms or risk factors are present.
  • Abnormal glucose in pregnancy: screen at 24–28 weeks with oral glucose tolerance test.
  • Aortic aneurysm & cardiovascular risk: smokers and hypertensive men: consider abdominal ultrasound and lipid profile.

Summary by age

  • 18–24 years: BP, BMI, STI, depression, HPV vaccine.
  • 25–39 years: add Pap, lipids if risk, HIV and hepatitis C once.
  • 40–49 years: add mammogram (≥40), diabetes screening, colorectal cancer screening (≥45).
  • 50–64 years: add colorectal, lung (if smoker), optional PSA, lipid monitoring.
  • 65+ years: add DEXA, fall/cognition assessment, vision and hearing checks.
